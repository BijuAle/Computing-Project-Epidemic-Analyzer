In the milieu of a humanitarian crisis (such as the 2014 Ebola epidemic), responders including public health personnels require epidemic data and its analysis. Such information is paramount for decision makers whose discernments and actions have direct impact on the control of the outbreak, consequently saving lives.

Aims

  • To build a system that can capture epidemic data on ground zero and provide analytics for provincial and national level decision makers.
  • To contribute to the understanding of public health practitioners and humanitarian agencies about an epidemic situation through the introduction of information technologies.

Objectives

  • To provide a robust data entry form that captures a broad range of health data dynamics.

  • To provide a graphical representation of data for better comprehension & decision making.

  • To learn about the impact of information technology in international humanitarian interventions.
  • To apply the principles from academic learnings to the real world scenarios and garner experience in research and development.
